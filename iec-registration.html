IEC Registration Guide (DGFT Portal)

Importer Exporter Code (IEC) is a 10-digit identification number issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT). Businesses involved in import or export activities must obtain IEC before starting international trade.

IEC is required for:

  • Importing goods into India
  • Exporting goods outside India
  • Receiving international payments
  • Clearing goods through customs

Technical Requirements

Before applying for IEC, ensure the following are available:

  • Valid PAN of the firm or individual
  • Valid mobile number and email ID
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) or Aadhaar e-sign
  • Active bank account in the name of the firm
  • Valid address proof

Documents Required for IEC

1. Business Registration Proof

  • Partnership deed
  • Trust deed
  • Society registration certificate
  • Certificate of incorporation
  • Other establishment proof

2. Address Proof

Any one of the following:

  • Sale deed
  • Rent agreement
  • Lease deed
  • Electricity bill
  • Telephone bill
  • Mobile postpaid bill

For proprietorship businesses, additional documents may include:

  • Aadhaar card
  • Passport
  • Voter ID

If the address proof is not in the firm's name, an NOC from the premises owner must be submitted.

3. Bank Account Proof

  • Cancelled cheque
  • Bank certificate

Bank account name should match the firm name.

Step-by-Step IEC Registration Process

Step 1 — Visit DGFT Portal

Go to the DGFT website:

https://dgft.gov.in

Step 2 — Register on DGFT Portal

Navigate:

Login → Register → Register as Importer / Exporter

Provide:

  • Name
  • Email ID
  • Mobile number

Verify OTP sent to mobile and email.

Step 3 — Login to DGFT Dashboard

After registration:

My Dashboard → Importer Exporter Code → Apply for IEC

Step 4 — Enter Business Details

Provide information such as:
  • Nature of firm
  • Firm name
  • PAN details
  • GSTIN (if applicable)
  • Business activities

Step 5 — Upload Documents

Upload required documents in:

  • PDF format
  • Maximum file size: 5MB

Step 6 — Sign Application

Application must be signed using:
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
  • Aadhaar e-Sign

Step 7 — Pay Application Fee

IEC application fee:

₹500

Payment is made through the BharatKosh payment gateway.

Step 8 — IEC Certificate Issued

After successful submission, IEC is usually issued immediately.

You can download the certificate from:

Services → IEC → Manage IEC → Print IEC

Important Notes

  • Bank account details are validated through NPCI.
  • Bank validation may take a few minutes or up to two weeks depending on the bank.
  • If validation is pending, application status may show Under Automatic Review.

IEC Renewal / Annual Update Process

The Import Export Code (IEC) issued by DGFT does not require renewal. However, as per DGFT regulations, IEC holders must confirm or update their IEC details annually between April and June. Failure to complete this update may result in automatic deactivation of the IEC.

Who Must Update IEC?

  • All IEC holders including proprietorships, partnerships, LLPs and companies
  • Importers and exporters registered with DGFT
  • Entities with active IEC even if no changes are required

Even if there are no changes in details, the IEC holder must log in and confirm the details every year.

Documents Required

  • IEC Number
  • PAN of the entity
  • Registered mobile number and email
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) or Aadhaar eSign
  • Updated business details (if applicable)

Step-by-Step IEC Update Process

Step 1 – Visit DGFT Website

Go to the official DGFT portal:
https://dgft.gov.in

Step 2 – Login to DGFT Portal

Click on Login and enter your registered email ID and password.

Step 3 – Navigate to IEC Profile

After login go to:
Services → IEC Profile Management → Manage IEC

Step 4 – Verify Details

Review all details including:

  • Business address
  • Bank account details
  • Directors / Partners
  • Branch details
Step 5 – Update or Confirm Details

If no changes are required, simply confirm the details and proceed. If changes are needed, update the information before submission.

Step 6 – Submit with DSC / Aadhaar eSign

Submit the update request using Digital Signature or Aadhaar eSign.

What Happens if IEC is Not Updated?

  • The IEC will be deactivated automatically
  • Importer / exporter cannot perform trade transactions
  • Customs clearance may be blocked
  • Benefits under export schemes may not be available

How to Reactivate Deactivated IEC

If IEC is deactivated due to non-update, the holder can reactivate it by completing the update process on the DGFT portal. Once the update is submitted successfully, the IEC will be reactivated.

Important Points

  • Annual IEC update must be completed between April and June every year.
  • No government fee is charged for confirming IEC details.
  • Any major changes may require supporting documents.

Conclusion

Although IEC does not require renewal, annual confirmation of IEC details is mandatory. Businesses involved in import and export should ensure their IEC profile remains updated to avoid disruptions in international trade activities.

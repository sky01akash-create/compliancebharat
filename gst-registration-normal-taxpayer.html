GST Registration for Normal Taxpayer – Complete Guide

GST registration is mandatory for businesses whose turnover exceeds the prescribed threshold limit or who are engaged in interstate supply of goods or services. Businesses registered under GST receive a unique Goods and Services Tax Identification Number (GSTIN).

Who Should Apply for GST Registration?

  • Businesses crossing GST turnover threshold
  • Interstate suppliers
  • E-commerce sellers
  • Businesses liable under reverse charge
  • Voluntary registration applicants

Eligibility for GST Registration

  • Valid PAN of business
  • Mobile number and email ID
  • Business address proof
  • Bank account details

Step-by-Step Process for GST Registration

Step 1: Visit GST Portal

Go to the official GST portal and click:

Services → Registration → New Registration

Step 2: Fill Part A of Application Enter details such as:
  • Legal name of business (as per PAN)
  • PAN number
  • Email address
  • Mobile number
  • State and district
Step 3: OTP Verification OTP will be sent to the registered email and mobile number for verification.
Step 4: Generate TRN After verification, a Temporary Reference Number (TRN) is generated which allows access to Part B of the application.
Step 5: Fill Part B Details Complete the application by filling details in the following sections:
  • Business Details
  • Promoter / Partner Details
  • Authorized Signatory
  • Principal Place of Business
  • Goods and Services
  • State Specific Information
Step 6: Upload Required Documents Upload documents such as:
  • PAN card
  • Business address proof
  • Photograph of promoters
  • Bank account details
Step 7: Aadhaar Authentication The GST portal may require Aadhaar authentication for verification.
Step 8: Application Verification Submit the application using:
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
  • E-sign
  • Electronic Verification Code (EVC)

Processing Time

GST registration is typically processed within **3–7 working days** after successful verification.

Documents Required

  • PAN card
  • Aadhaar card
  • Business registration certificate
  • Address proof of business
  • Bank account proof
